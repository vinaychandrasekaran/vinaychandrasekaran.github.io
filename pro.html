Approach to (Glomerular) Proteinuria

Nephritic Syndrome

Pathophysiology & Clinical Features

  • Inflammation → glomerular damage → renal failure, protein loss, blood loss
  • Renal failure:
    • ↑ BUN & Cr, oliguria
    • Hypertension & edema (↑ hydrostatic pressure)
  • Proteinuria (<3.5 g/day):
    • Hypertension & edema (↓ oncotic pressure)
  • Blood loss:
    • Cola-colored urine (melena of GU tract)
    • Dysmorphic RBCs
    • RBC casts (pathognomonic)

Diagnosis

  • Initial workup:
    • Thorough H&P
    • Urinalysis (hematuria, proteinuria)
    • BMP (azotemia)
  • Exclude nephrotic range:
    • 24-h urine protein
    • Urine protein:creatinine ratio
  • Narrow differential by complement status
  • Gold standard: kidney biopsy (EM, IF, LM)

Treatment

  • ACE/ARB
  • Immunosuppression

Nephrotic Syndrome

Pathophysiology & Clinical Features

  • Glomerular damage → selective protein loss
  • Frothy urine (protein = surfactant)
  • Ankle/facial edema (albumin loss)
  • Hypertension (third spacing → ↓ renal flow → RAAS)
  • Hyperlipidemia (>300) → fatty casts/oval fat bodies
  • Hypercoagulability (ATIII loss)
  • Recurrent infections (IgG loss)

Diagnosis

  • Initial workup:
    • Urinalysis (proteinuria)* MM may have negative UA
    • Lipid panel
    • CMP (albumin)
  • Confirmation:
    • 24-h urine protein (>3.5 g/day)
    • Protein:Cr ratio (>3.5:1)
  • Gold standard: kidney biopsy (EM, IF, LM)

Treatment

  • Statin
  • ACE/ARB
  • Sodium restriction

RPGN (Crescentic)

  • Rapid ESRD in months if untreated
  • Presents with AKI, anorexia / fatigue, nephritis
  • Type I: linear (Goodpasture)
  • Type II: granular (PSGN, lupus nephritis)
  • Type III: pauci-immune (ANCA)

Treatment: steroids & cyclophosphamide

Histopath Derivation

  • “Membranous”: EM: subepithelial deposits; LM: basement membrane thickening
  • “Proliferative”: LM: mesangial hypercellularity;
  • Pathophys = immune complex: IF: granular, lumpy bumpy
  • RPGN: LM: crescents
